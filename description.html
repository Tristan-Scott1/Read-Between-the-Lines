Read Between the Lines

Tristan Scott

For this project, I began by trying to figure out what kind of compositions I wanted to create.

Immediately, I was drawn to typography posters for inspiration.

For my animations, I initially flirted with ideas like a printing press or screen printing, where new layers of color are added in a swiping motion, or even a factory line where new pieces were added with machine arms or other automation.

However, when visually researching ideas for what posters I wanted to take inspiration from, I came across some Swiss designs that I enjoyed. I became interested in tackling the challenge of how I could create some unity between some unique and visually interesting posters, to make a piece that felt whole but still had three distinct compositions that each experimented with animation in unique and interesting ways.

The one poster I was inspired by made me think of the line "read between the lines," and I wanted to roll with that. I ended up taking some more inspiration from a poem by Brian Bilston and used that as the unifying framework of the compositions.

Overall, I had a great time experimenting with variable typography and CSS animation in creative and expressive ways. I appreciate poster design, and it was fun to explore that in web format.

Thank you for checking out my project!
